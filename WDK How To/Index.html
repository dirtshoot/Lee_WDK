Section 3 - Configuration data.php
Information about your theme is stored in your data.php, along with various amounts of other information required to render the theme correctly. Below you will see a list of theme variables used to provide the user with information about the theme. Editing additional information not listed below in your data.php may cause your theme to be rendered incorrectly.

$Theme_name = "WDK Original Blue & Silver";
$Theme_version = "1.0.0";
$Theme_author = "Lee Tennant";
$Theme_compatible = "Dafsim WDK System";
$Theme_readme = "readme.txt";
$Theme_Thumbnail = "Images/Thumbnail.jpg";
$Theme_Stylesheet = "Style.css";
$Theme_Index = "index.php";
$Theme_description = "WDK Original Blue & Silver theme, created to give you...";

Section 6 - Configuration style.css
Cascading Style Sheets (.css) Is the most common application for styleing web pages written in HTML and XHTML. Your stylesheet used for elements such as the layout, colors, and fonts.

Example of a entry into your Style.css

#selector [, selector2, ...][:pseudo-class] {
property: value;
property2: value2;
property3: value3;
...etc (as required)
}

/* comment*/

Visit: http://www.w3schools.com/Css/css_intro.asp for additional information on Cascading Style Sheets!
Section 7 - Setup readme.txt
Creating a readme.txt file is required and very handy for your theme. Readme.txt is used to keep track of but not limited to, bugs, changes and or requirements. Althrough this file is required, you may or may not decide to keep it updated.
Section 8 - Applying your new theme
Once you have completed your theme, you will need to upload your main theme folder to the "Themes" folder. Once you have successfully uploaded your theme. Open your control panel - "Themes" section. If no errors are reported. You will now be able to see your newly installed theme listed in the themes. To apply your new theme click on the "Set" icon to the right of your theme's thumbnail.
